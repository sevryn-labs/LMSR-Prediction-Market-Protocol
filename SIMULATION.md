# Agent-Based Simulation System

The simulation engine is a core part of this repository, enabling the study of market behavior before deploying to the mainnet.

## Trader Beliefs

Each agent in the simulation is assigned a **private probability belief** $p_i \in [0, 1]$ for each outcome $i$.
- **Belief Distribution**: Beliefs are typically generated by adding Gaussian noise to a "hidden truth" probability.
- **Informational Edge**: An agent perceives an opportunity whenever their belief $p_i$ differs significantly from the market price $P_i$.

## Trading Strategies

The simulator supports various strategies for agents:

### 1. Simple Arbitrage (Implemented)
The agent buys shares of outcome $i$ if $p_i > P_i + \epsilon$, where $\epsilon$ is a minimum profit threshold (e.g., 2%).
- **Trade Volume**: The agent invests capital proportional to the size of the gap $(p_i - P_i)$.

### 2. Kelly Criterion (Experimental)
A more advanced strategy where agents size their trades to maximize the expected growth rate of their wealth.

### 3. Noise Trading
Random trades designed to simulate market activity and test the robustness of information aggregation against non-informative signals.

## Price Convergence

The primary goal of the simulation is to observe **Information Aggregation**.
- **The Convergence Property**: If participants are rational and have independent noisy signals about the truth, the LMSR price should converge to the mean of the agents' beliefs.
- **Example**: If the true probability is 70% and 50 agents have beliefs ranging from 60% to 80%, the market price will move toward 70% as they trade.

## Experimental Setups

Researchers can customize the simulation in `cli.ts` or a custom script:
- `numAgents`: Number of participating traders.
- `numRounds`: Number of discrete trading opportunities.
- `b`: The liquidity parameter. Low $b$ results in faster convergence but higher volatility. High $b$ requires more trades for the price to move.

## Observation Tools

The CLI provides a visual round-by-round update of:
- Current market prices.
- Convergence match percentage relative to the average agent belief.
- Price stability metrics.
